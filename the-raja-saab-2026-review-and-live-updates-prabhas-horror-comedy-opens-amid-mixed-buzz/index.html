The Raja Saab 2026 Review & Live Updates: Prabhas’ Horror-Comedy Opens Amid Mixed Buzz

Parsh January 9, 2026 Trending

The year 2026 began with a bang in Indian cinema as The Raja Saab, starring Prabhas, released in theatres nationwide on January 9. The film, directed by Maruthi, was highly anticipated because of Prabhas’ massive fan following and its festive Sankranti release. Even before the release, advance bookings showed solid traction in southern states, and the film generated major hype online.

At the end of the day, fans eagerly awaited the mix of horror, fantasy, and comedy promised by the makers.

Why This Matters

From the very first trailers, audiences were intrigued by the palace setting, supernatural elements, and humorous twists. Really, the film was positioned as a family-friendly horror-comedy with a fantasy twist rather than a straight scare-fest. This strategy built anticipation of an entertaining yet lighthearted cinematic experience.

The reality is, early chatter suggested that while viewers were excited, they were also curious if the film could live up to the promise of Prabhas’ star power combined with a unique storytelling experiment.

Advance Bookings and Pre-Release Buzz

In Andhra Pradesh and Telangana, advance ticket sales were particularly strong, with several theatres reporting near-full occupancy even before the official release. In comparison, other regions, including the Hindi-speaking belt, had more moderate bookings.

While the movie had a strong start in the south, it would rely heavily on word-of-mouth to sustain performance in other parts of India, analysts noted. The initial interest was also reflected in early social media discussions, with fans debating expectations, plot teasers, and character reveals.

The Sankranti release window usually boosts family-oriented films, and The Raja Saab benefited from this timing. That said, the disparity in regional pre-sales highlighted that while Prabhas’ presence drew viewers, the horror-comedy blend remained a question mark for some audiences.

Critical Reception: A Mixed Bag

Once the movie premiered, early reviews painted a split picture. Critics appreciated Prabhas’ screen presence, noting that he brought charisma and energy to scenes that might have otherwise fallen flat. The emotional climax and some humorous sequences were also highlighted as engaging moments.

However, several reviewers criticized the screenplay for being uneven and the pacing inconsistent. The blend of horror, fantasy, and comedy did not always gel, leaving some scenes feeling forced or disjointed. The comedic beats, while entertaining at times, did not always land effectively, and certain plot points felt underdeveloped.

Overall, the critical consensus suggested that the film’s concept was promising, but the execution lacked cohesion in parts.

Audience Reactions and Social Media Buzz

The audience response reflected a similar split. Social media platforms were buzzing with opinions ranging from enthusiastic support to sharp critique. Many viewers expressed disappointment in the film’s storytelling, feeling that the horror elements were predictable and the comedic moments inconsistent. Comments such as “confusing narration” and “lost potential” appeared frequently in online discussions.

Conversely, a section of fans appreciated Prabhas’ performance, the visual spectacle of the palace setting, and the emotional payoffs in the second half. They noted that despite narrative shortcomings, the film offered moments of entertainment and spectacle that made it worth watching.

This split in reaction created substantial online conversation, keeping The Raja Saab trending across platforms in the days following its release.

Controversies and Extra Buzz

Adding to the movie’s online chatter was a controversy regarding alleged attempts to influence reviews. While the claims were unverified, the discussion fueled further engagement and debate among fans.

In addition, discussions on fan forums and social media highlighted the differing expectations between die-hard supporters and casual viewers, especially regarding the horror-comedy blend.

Technical Aspects and Production

The film’s technical aspects drew mixed reactions. The visual effects, intended to create a magical and eerie palace environment, were praised for ambition but criticized for inconsistency. While certain sequences showcased impressive production design, others fell short, affecting the overall immersive experience.

Cinematography captured the grandeur of the setting, and the background score supported emotional beats, though some viewers felt it did little to elevate the suspense or horror.

Music, composed to complement both comedic and dramatic moments, received a lukewarm response. While certain tracks were energetic, they did not leave a lasting impression, and reviewers noted that the soundtrack failed to significantly enhance the storytelling.

Box Office Performance and Projections

Initial box office collections suggest a decent start in southern states, particularly Andhra Pradesh and Telangana, where Prabhas’ fan base is strongest. Domestic earnings were bolstered by the festive period, though early estimates indicated a slower start in other regions.

Analysts noted that the film’s long-term success would depend heavily on word-of-mouth and repeat viewership, particularly outside the core southern market. Internationally, the film garnered attention in key overseas markets, with early ticket sales showing strong interest. However, sustained success would rely on audience reception once reviews and feedback spread globally.

Audience Takeaways

Despite mixed reactions, The Raja Saab managed to spark conversation, which is often the hallmark of a high-profile release. For some, it offered entertainment, emotional moments, and comic relief. For others, it highlighted missed opportunities in storytelling and execution.

The film’s divided response reflects a growing trend where audiences are more critical and discerning, analyzing both narrative coherence and technical performance. Online discussions demonstrate passionate engagement from both fans and general moviegoers. Negative reactions keep the film in public discourse, while positive feedback on Prabhas’ performance and select sequences helps retain interest. This dynamic ensures that the film stays relevant in conversation well beyond its opening weekend.

Conclusion: A Mixed but Memorable Start to 2026

The Raja Saab opened 2026 with significant attention and anticipation. It is a film that, despite flaws, demonstrates the power of star appeal, festive timing, and a unique genre approach in attracting audiences.

While critical and audience reactions are mixed, moments of entertainment, spectacle, and emotional resonance provide reasons for fans to engage with the film.

The ultimate fate of the movie in terms of box office success will likely depend on word-of-mouth, regional appeal, and the ability of the horror-comedy blend to resonate with diverse audiences.

Whether it becomes a memorable festival hit, a sleeper success, or a polarizing entry in Prabhas’ career, The Raja Saab has ensured it remains one of the most talked-about films of early 2026.
